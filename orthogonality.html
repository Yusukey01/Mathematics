Inner Product

The inner product of vectors \(u, v \in \mathbb{R}^n \) is a single scalar \[u \cdot v = u^Tv = u_1v_1 + \cdots + u_nv_n.\]
Now we need the definition of the "length" in higher dimension. The norm of a vector \(v \in \mathbb{R}^n\) is the nonnegative scalar \[ \|v\| = \sqrt{v \cdot v}.\] Also, a vector whose length is 1 is called a unit vector \[u = \frac{v}{\|v\|}\] that maintains its "direction." We call this process normalization, which is useful in many applications such as machine learning.
