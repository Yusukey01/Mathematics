Inner Product

The inner product of vectors \(u, v \in \mathbb{R}^n \) is a single scalar \[u \cdot v = u^Tv = u_1v_1 + \cdots + u_nv_n.\]
We need the definition of the "length" in higher dimension. The norm of a vector \(v \in \mathbb{R}^n\) is the nonnegative scalar \[ \|v\| = \sqrt{v \cdot v}.\] Also, a vector whose length is 1 is called a unit vector \[u = \frac{v}{\|v\|}\] that maintains its "direction." We call this process normalization, which is useful in many applications such as machine learning. Since we got a "length," now we can define the distance between vectors \(u, v \in \mathbb{R}^n \) as \[dist(u, v) = \|u - v\|. \]
Geometrically, \(dist(u, v) \text{ and } dist(u, -v)\) are perpendicular iff they are the same. \[\|u - v\|^2 = (u-v) \cdot (u-v) = u \cdots (u-v) + (-v) \cdot (u - v) = \|u\|^2 - 2u \cdot v + \|v\|^2\] \[\|u - (-v)\|^2 = (u+v) \cdot (u+v) = u \cdots (u+v) + (v) \cdot (u + v) = \|u\|^2 + 2u \cdot v + \|v\|^2\] You might notice that these are close to the Pythagorean theorem:\(\|u - (-v)\|^2 = \|u\|^2 + \|v\|^2 \) iff \(u\) and \(v\) are orthogonal. So, we need \(- 2u \cdot v = + 2u \cdot v\) for the general orthogonality as follows.
Two vectors \(u, v \in \mathbb{R}^n \) are orthogonal to each other if their inner product \(u \cdot v =0\).
