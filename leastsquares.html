Least-Squares Problems

If \(A \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}\) and \(b \in \mathbb{R}^m\), a least-squares solution of \(Ax = b\) is an \(\hat{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n\) s.t. \[\forall x \in \mathbb{R}^n, \quad \| b - A\hat{x} \| \leq \| b - Ax \|. \]

In practice, it is possible that a solution of some problem \(Ax = b\) is required but it does not exist (= the system is inconsistent). In that case, we try to find an \(x\) that makes \(Ax\) close to \(b\) as much as possible.
