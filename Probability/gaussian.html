Normal Distribution

A random variable \(X\) has a normal(Gaussian) distribution with parameters \(\mu \) and \(\sigma^2\) if its p.d.f. is given by \[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}}e^{\frac{-(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \qquad x \in (-\infty, \infty) \] where \(\mu \in (-\infty, \infty)\) and \(\sigma > 0\) and it is denoted as \(X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)\).


The above figure shows the normal p.d.f. curve. \(\mu\) is the center, and \(\sigma\) is the distance from the center to the inflection point of the curve. Thus, \[ \mathbb{E }[X] = \mu \qquad \text{Ver }(X) = \sigma^2. \]
