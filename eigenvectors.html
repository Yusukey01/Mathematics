Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues

An eigenvector of an \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) is a nonzero vector \(x\) s.t. \[Ax = \lambdax \tag{1}\] for some scalar \(\lambda\) that is called an eigenvalue of \(A\) id there is a nontrivial solution \(x\) of (1). We call such an \(x\) an eigenvector corresponding to \(\lambda\).
