Linear Transformation

A transformation (or mapping) \(T: \mathbb{R}^m \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n \) is linear if
\(\forall u, v \in\mathbb{R}^m,\) and any scalars \(c\), \[T(u+v) = T(u) +T(v)\] \[T(cu) = cT(u).\]
For example, consider a matrix \(A \in \mathbb{R}^(m \times 3)\) and a vector (\x \in \mathbb{R}^3 \). The matrix-vector product \(Ax\) is actually a linear transformation \(T: x \mapsto Ax\). Say, (\u, v \in \mathbb{R}^3 \) and \(c\) is a scalar. \[ \begin{align} A(u+v) = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} u_1 + v_1 \\ u_2 + v_3 \\ u_3 + v_3 \end{bmatrix} = (u_1 + v_1)a_1 + (u_2 + v_2)a_2 + (u_3 + v_3)a_3 = (u_1a_1 + u_2a_2 + u_3a_3) + (v_1a_1 + v_2a_2 + v_3a_3) = Au + Av \end{align} \] \[ \begin{align} A(cu) = \begin{bmatrix} a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} cu_1\\ cu_2 \\ cu_3 \end{bmatrix} = c(u_1a_1)+c(u_2a_2)+c(u_3a_3) = c(u_1a_1 + u_2a_2 + u_3a_3) = c(Au) \end{align} \] The linear transformation is one of the most fundamental idea in mathematics.
