Linear Transformation

A transformation (or mapping) \(T: \mathbb{R}^m \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n \) is linear if
\(\forall u, v \in\mathbb{R}^m,\) and any scalars \(c\), \[T(u+v) = T(u) +T(v)\] \[T(cu) = cT(u)\]. For example, consider a matrix (\A \in \mathbb{R}^(m \times 3) \) and a vector (\x \in \mathbb{R}^3 \) Matrix Consider a matrix transformation \(x \mapsto Ax\). The linear transformation is one of the most fundamental idea in mathematics.
