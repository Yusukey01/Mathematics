Linear Approximations

Linear approximation refers to the process of approximation a function (\f(x)\) near a point \(x_o\) using a linear function. It is used to simplify complex functions locally. \[ L(x) = f(x_o) + f'(x_o)(x -x_o) \] where \(f'(x_o) = \lim_{x\to x_o}\frac{f(x)-f(x_o)}{x-x_o}\) is derivative at \(x_o\).
\(L(x)\) is called the linearization of \(f(x)\) at \(x_o\).
