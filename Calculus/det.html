Derivative of the Frobenius norm

Tthe Frobenius norm of a matrix \(X \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}\) is defined as: \[ f(X) = \| X \|_F = \sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)} \] Now, taking the differential \(df\):
First, by the chain rule: \[ df = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}d[\text{tr }(X^TX)] \] Note: \[ \begin{align*} d(\text{tr }(A)) &= \text{tr }(A + dA) - \text{tr }(A) \\\\\ &= \text{tr }(A) + \text{tr }(dA) - \text{tr }(A) \\\\ &= \text{tr }(dA) \end{align*} \] Thus: \[ df = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }[d(X^TX)] \] By the product rule: \[ \begin{align*} df &= \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }[dX^TX + X^TdX]\\\\ &= \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }(dX^TX) + \text{tr }(X^TdX) \end{align*} \] Since \(\text{tr }(dX^TX) = \text{tr }(X^TdX)\), \[ df = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}2\text{tr }(X^TdX)\\\\ \]
