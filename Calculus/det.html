Derivative of the Frobenius norm

The Frobenius norm of a matrix \(X \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}\) is defined as: \[ f(X) = \| X \|_F = \sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)} \] Now, taking the differential \(df\):

First, by the chain rule: \[ df = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}d[\text{tr }(X^TX)] \] Note: \[ \begin{align*} d(\text{tr }(A)) &= \text{tr }(A + dA) - \text{tr }(A) \\\\\ &= \text{tr }(A) + \text{tr }(dA) - \text{tr }(A) \\\\ &= \text{tr }(dA) \end{align*} \] Thus: \[ df = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }[d(X^TX)] \] By the product rule: \[ \begin{align*} df &= \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }[dX^TX + X^TdX]\\\\ &= \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }(dX^TX) + \text{tr }(X^TdX) \end{align*} \] Since \(\text{tr }(dX^TX) = \text{tr }((dX^TX)^T) = \text{tr }(X^TdX)\), \[ \begin{align*} df &= \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}2\text{tr }(X^TdX)\\\\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\text{tr }(X^TdX) \end{align*} \] Here, \(\text{tr }(X^TdX)\) represents the Frobenius inner product of matrices \(X\) and \(dX\). Then: \[ df = \frac{X}{\sqrt{\text{tr }(X^TX)}}\cdot dX \tag{1} \] Therefore, \[ \nabla f = \frac{X}{ \| X \|_F}. \]
The operation at (1) might not be familiar. Let's consider one more simple example.
Consider \(f(A) = x^TAy\) where \(A\) is a \(m \times n\) matrix, \(x \in \mathbb{R}^m\), and \(y \in \mathbb{R}^n\). By the product rule, \[ df = x^TdAy \] Since \(df\) is a scalar, taking the trace of \(x^TdAy\) is not change the value. Then: \[ df = \text{tr }(x^TdAy) \] By the cyclic property of trace: \[ df = \text{tr }(yx^TdA) \] Therefore, \[ \nabla f = (yx^T)^T = xy^T \]
